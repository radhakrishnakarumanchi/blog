Leaving Udhyam Vyapaar - Naive Quitting

I recently left Udhyam Vyapaar. Almost a year before that, I talked to my manager about my desire to leave. I didn’t have a timeline in my mind; I felt that developing a successor to lead Vyapaar was a thing I ought to do before leaving.

I hoped that one of the tenured team members could step into my shoes and lead Vyapaar. For some reason, I couldn’t progress much on that front, and the feeling that I ought to develop a successor remained strong. I didn’t know the source of that feeling, though.

Nonetheless, I am happy that I actively contributed to the search, assessment, and onboarding of my replacement. The new leader is Menaka; she is an excellent fit for Vyapaar, and I was glad she and I could spend 30+ hours one-on-one while she was settling in.

I have some time on my hands now and am reading a bit. I happened to re-read an article titled ‘Naive Quitting’, and in doing so, I had an epiphany regarding my urge to develop a successor.

Do read Naive Quitting to understand my urge; it will not take more than couple of minutes.

My epiphany is this: I had no conscious awareness of the naive-quitting notion and yet somehow seem to have driven by it. I do not know whether I’ve quit naively or not, but I am happy I went about it the way I did.
